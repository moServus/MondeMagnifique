# Wien (Vienna) - Wikipedia Article Summary

Source: https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wien

## Overview
Vienna is Austria's federal capital and one of nine federal states. With over 2 million inhabitants, it ranks as the second-largest city in the German-speaking world and the fifth-largest in the European Union.

## Key Facts

**Geographic Location & Size:**
- Area: 414.82 km² (smallest Austrian state)
- Located at the eastern edge of the Alps
- Elevation ranges from 151m (Lobau) to 544m (Hermannskogel)
- Completely surrounded by Lower Austria

**Population & Demographics:**
- 2025 population: 2,028,289
- Metropolitan area: ~2.97 million (about one-third of Austria's total)
- Population density: 4,889 per km²
- Foreign-born residents: 35.4% (as of January 2024)
- Migration background: 50.3% (average 2023)

## Historical Significance

The city's name derives from the Wien River. The etymology traces to Celtic-Romance "Vedunia" (forest stream). Vienna held the designation of imperial capital and residence during the Austro-Hungarian Empire and developed into a major European cultural and political center.

## Climate

Vienna exhibits a transitional climate with oceanic and continental influences. The inner city experiences the warmest conditions in Austria, with average temperatures around 11.4°C. Annual precipitation averages approximately 600mm, with regional variation from 514.5mm (east) to 741.5mm (west).

## Administrative Division

Vienna comprises 23 municipal districts (since 1954), each with distinct characteristics:
- Inner districts (3-9) bounded by the Gürtel
- Danube Canal and Danube form boundaries for multiple districts
- Districts organized with postal codes 1010-1230

## Cultural & Economic Importance

- Host to over 40 international organizations including OPEC and IAEA
- UNESCO World Heritage sites include the historic center and Schönbrunn Palace
- Annual tourism: 8+ million visitors
- BIP pro capita: 61,900 euros (2024)
- Major architectural styles: Baroque, Jugendstil, Gründerzeit

## Natural Features

The city contains diverse flora and fauna:
- 22 of Austria's 29 bat species
- Protected areas including Nationalpark Donau-Auen and Biosphärenpark Wienerwald
- Approximately 50% greenland coverage
- 28.4% garden areas, 17.8% forests

## Climate Change Projections

By 2050, Vienna's climate may resemble contemporary Skopje, while temperatures could reach Mediterranean levels by century's end, affecting infrastructure and biodiversity.
